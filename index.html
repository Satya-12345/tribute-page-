A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, full name Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, a small town in Tamil Nadu, India. He hailed from a humble background and grew up in a financially modest family. Kalam showed early interest and aptitude in science and engineering. He pursued his education at Saint Joseph's College in Tiruchirappalli, where he studied physics. After completing his undergraduate studies, he moved to Madras (now Chennai) to study aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT). He specialized in Aeronautical Engineering and graduated in 1958. The term "Missile Man of India" refers to Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, who was a renowned scientist and served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Dr. Kalam was often referred to as the "Missile Man of India" because of his significant contributions to the development of India's missile and space technology programs. During his career at the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), Dr. Kalam played a pivotal role in the development of various missile systems, including the Agni and Prithvi series of ballistic missiles. He also contributed to the successful development of India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle, the SLV-3, and the operationalization of the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle (GSLV). Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was widely respected for his scientific expertise, visionary leadership, and commitment to advancing India's missile and space programs. He was not only a scientist but also a great teacher, writer, and motivator who inspired many young minds in India and around the world.
